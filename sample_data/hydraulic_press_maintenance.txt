# Hydraulic Press - Maintenance Manual

## 1. Preventive Maintenance Schedule

### Daily Checks
- Check hydraulic fluid levels in the reservoir.
- Inspect all hoses and fittings for signs of leakage.
- Verify that the safety light curtains are functioning correctly.

### Weekly Checks
- Lubricate guide rails and columns.
- Check system operating pressure (should not exceed 350 bar).
- Inspect the main cylinder seals for seepage.

### Monthly Checks
- Replace hydraulic oil filters.
- Take a fluid sample for contamination analysis.
- Inspect electrical connections and tighten if necessary.

## 2. Troubleshooting Guide

### Issue: Loss of Pressure
**Probable Causes:**
1. Low hydraulic fluid level.
2. Clogged oil filter (HF-200).
3. Worn main cylinder seals.
4. Faulty pressure relief valve.

**Corrective Actions:**
- Check fluid level and top up if necessary.
- Replace oil filter.
- If seals are worn, order Replacement Kit HS-400 and schedule downtime for repair.

### Issue: Overheating
**Probable Causes:**
1. Continuous operation beyond rated duty cycle.
2. Malfunctioning heat exchanger or cooling fan.
3. Degraded hydraulic fluid.

**Corrective Actions:**
- Allow the machine to cool down.
- Inspect the heat exchanger and clean if clogged.
- Replace the hydraulic fluid if it appears dark or smells burnt.
