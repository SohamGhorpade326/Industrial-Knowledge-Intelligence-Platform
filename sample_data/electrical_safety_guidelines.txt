# Plant Electrical Safety Guidelines

## 1. General Electrical Safety
- Only authorized and trained personnel (certified electricians) are permitted to perform electrical repairs.
- All electrical panels must have a minimum clearance of 3 feet (1 meter) in front of them.
- Do not use extension cords as a substitute for permanent wiring.
- Any exposed or frayed wiring must be reported immediately and tagged out.

## 2. Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) Procedure
Before performing any maintenance on electrically powered equipment:
1. Identify all energy sources (electrical, pneumatic, hydraulic).
2. Shut down the equipment using the standard operating procedure.
3. Isolate the equipment from all energy sources.
4. Apply a designated lock and tag to the energy-isolating device.
5. Verify isolation by attempting to start the equipment.

## 3. Required PPE for Electrical Work
Depending on the voltage level and arc flash hazard category, the following may be required:
- Insulated gloves with leather protectors (Class 0 or higher).
- Arc-rated long-sleeve shirts and pants.
- Safety glasses and arc-rated face shield.
- Electrical hazard (EH) rated safety shoes.
- Hearing protection.

## 4. Emergency Response (Electrical Shock)
1. DO NOT touch the victim if they are still in contact with the electrical source.
2. De-energize the circuit immediately (turn off the breaker or main disconnect).
3. Call the Emergency Response Team or dial 108 for an ambulance.
4. Once the area is safe, begin CPR if the victim is unresponsive.
